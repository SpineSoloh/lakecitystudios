Music Theory and Composition Modules

Music Theory and Composition courses encompass a variety of modules designed to deepen understanding of music fundamentals and enhance composition skills. Click on the modules below to expand and view details:

Music Theory Modules

Notation: Understanding musical symbols, staffs, clefs, and basic rhythmic values.

Rhythm and Meter: Exploring different time signatures, note values, and rhythmic patterns.

Major and Minor Scales: Learning the construction and characteristics of major, natural minor, harmonic minor, and melodic minor scales.

Intervals: Identifying and building intervals, understanding their qualities (perfect, major, minor, augmented, diminished).

Triads and Seventh Chords: Building and identifying triads and various seventh chords.

Chord Progressions: Understanding common chord progressions and their functions (e.g., I-IV-V-I).

Key Signatures: Recognizing and writing key signatures.

Modulation: Techniques for changing keys within a piece.

Melodic Construction: Creating melodies using scales and intervals.

Phrase and Form: Analyzing musical forms (binary, ternary, sonata form, etc.) and understanding phrases.

Species Counterpoint: Learning the rules of counterpoint in different species (first species, second species, etc.).

Contrapuntal Techniques: Exploring techniques like imitation, inversion, and retrograde.

Musical Textures: Understanding different textures (monophonic, homophonic, polyphonic) and their effects.

Instrumentation and Timbre: Studying the characteristics of different instruments and how they contribute to texture.

Formal Analysis: Analyzing pieces of music for form, harmony, and melody.

Historical Context: Understanding the historical and cultural background of different musical styles.

Composition Modules

Writing Melodies: Developing skills in crafting memorable melodies.

Chord Progressions: Learning how to create effective harmonic structures.

Instrument Roles: Understanding how different instruments function in an ensemble.

Score Preparation: Learning how to prepare a score for various instrumentations.

Song Structure: Understanding typical song forms and the relationship between lyrics and music.

Writing Effective Lyrics: Techniques for writing compelling and singable lyrics.

Thematic Development: Techniques for developing and transforming musical ideas.

Using Non-Traditional Scales: Exploring modes, pentatonic scales, and microtonality.

DAWs (Digital Audio Workstations): Introduction to using software for composing and producing music.

Notation Software: Learning how to use notation software like Sibelius or Finale for scoring compositions.

Basic Recording Techniques: Understanding how to record and produce music effectively.

Mixing and Mastering: Learning the basics of mixing tracks for clarity and cohesion.

Workshops and Peer Review: Engaging in group critiques to develop and refine compositions.

Collaborative Projects: Opportunities to collaborate with other musicians, lyricists, or composers.

Creating a Portfolio: Compiling works to showcase your composition skills.

Performance Opportunities: Finding opportunities to have works performed or recorded.
